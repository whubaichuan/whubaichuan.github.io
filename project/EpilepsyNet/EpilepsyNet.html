Abstract

Epilepsy is a mental disease that affects more than 65 million people, around 1% of the human on the earth. The medical monitoring system, mainly based on the international 10-20 system with a negative effect on social stigma, is used to detect the onset of a seizure and call for some first-aid help due to the drug resistance. To develop a high-performing seizure detection algorithm, deep learning technology is introduced to extract the potential features among EEG signals nowadays. But training a deep neural network needs lots of data, especially the seizure data. For a real new patient, how can we construct a patient-specific seizure detection system quickly and effectively is a pressing issue that needs to be addressed. In this paper, we propose the firstly end-to-end real unsupervised network, called EpilepsyNet, without any need for seizure data. Especially, records of non-seizure data in only 20 minutes on our e-glass system are enough for EpilepsyNet's training and testing. Our experiments in CHB-MIT dataset show that our generated seizure data has comparable detection results, the geometric mean of 78\%, as the real seizure data. EpilepsyNet is a powerful solution for continuous and non-invasive seizure detection, especially for the new patients without any seizure data recorded.
