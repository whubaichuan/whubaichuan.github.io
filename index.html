Research

Pulished papers are listed below.

EpilepsyNet: Interpretable Self-Supervised Seizure Detection for Low-Power Wearable Systems
Baichuan Huang, Renato Zanetti, Azra Abtahi Fahliani, David Atienza, Amir Aminifar
Accepted by IEEE AICAS 2023
paper/ ppt

We propose the first interpretable self-supervised network for seizure detection without any need for real seizure data in training, which has comparable performance with supervised methods. (Contemporaneous and similar work of peers in AAAI2023)

Lightweight Machine Learning for Seizure Detection on Wearable Devices
Baichuan Huang, Azra Abtahi Fahliani, Amir Aminifar
Accepted by IEEE ICASSP 2023
Top-5 Ranking in the Seizure Detection Challenge
paper/ ppt

We propose a lightweight machine-learning framework for real-time epilepsy monitoring on wearable devices (SensorDot of Byteflies).

M3VSNet: Unsupervised Multi-metric Multi-view Stereo Network
Baichuan Huang, Hongwei Yi, Can Huang, Yijia He, Jingbin Liu, Xiao Liu
Accepted by IEEE ICIP 2021
paper/ code/ ppt/ poster/

This method establishes the state-of-the-arts unsupervised MVS method and demonstrates the powerful generalization ability.

A Survey of Simultaneous Localization and Mapping with an Envision in 6G Wireless Networks
Baichuan Huang, Jun Zhao, Jingbin Liu
Journal of Global Positioning Systems , 2021
paper/ project/

The paper makes an overview in SLAM including Lidar SLAM, visual SLAM, and their fusion.

A Robust Indoor Positioning Method based on Bluetooth Low Energy with Separate Channel Information
Baichuan Huang, Jingbin Liu, Wei Sun, Fan Yang
Sensors, 2019
paper

To improve the adaptability and robustness of the BLE positioning system, we propose making full use of the three separate channels instead of their combination.

AOA Estimation Based on Channel State Information Extracted from WiFi with Double Antenna
Jingbin Liu, Baichuan Huang, Bin Zhang
GEOMATICS AND INFORMATION SCIENCE OF WUHAN UNIVERS, 2018
paper

This paper uses two virtual antennas to realize the music algorithm (Angle of Arrival) based on channel state information (CSI).
